Ad Template Structure

Every ad follows this structure. The hook is the variable (unlimited angles). The body video is the constant (product feature). The text on the body resolves the hook's premise.

Hook
VARIABLE

Creates emotional tension. The viewer thinks "wait, that's me" or "wait, what?"

Visual: Person, scene, setting — NOT the product yet
Text: 5-20 words. Creates the PREMISE — an unresolved tension
Duration: 3-5s (text-overlay) / 7-14s (VO)

This is where ALL creative variety lives. Unlimited angles, emotions, patterns.

→
Bridge
IMPLICIT

The psychological WHY connecting hook tension to product resolution.

Text-overlay: Lives in the body text — MUST answer WHY the product resolves the hook
VO+caption: Lives in the voiceover — last hook sentence + first body sentence

Without a bridge, hook and body feel like two unrelated ads stitched together.

→
Body
CONSTANT VIDEO · VARIABLE TEXT

Resolves the hook's tension through the product. The viewer sees the feature working.

Visual: Screen recording / product demo (ALWAYS real footage, reusable)
Text: Resolves the hook's premise — product reveals itself through the resolution
Duration: 5s

NOT a product description. NOT "Netflix for Bible content." The text completes the hook's thought.

→
CTA
FIXED

Action verb only.

Examples: "Download Free" / "Try It Free" / "Tap Below"
Duration: 2-3s

How it goes wrong (TV Commercial style)

Hook
"VBS prep used to take 20 hours. this year? 2 hours. done."
Creates tension: HOW?
→
Body (BAD)
"Bible Netflix that kids beg to watch. Works on any device."
Generic product description. Doesn't answer HOW. Disconnected.

How it should work (Copywriting)

Hook
"VBS prep used to take 20 hours. this year? 2 hours. done."
Creates tension: HOW?
→
Body (GOOD)
"One app. Every VBS episode queued up. Kids sit down, hit play."
Resolves the premise. Product emerges through the answer.

Another example

Hook
"my kids know every Marvel character but can't name a single apostle"
Identity tension: I'm failing as a parent
→
Body (GOOD)
"now they binge Bible stories the same way — and actually remember them"
Resolves: kids engage with faith content like they do entertainment

Delivery Modes

Text Overlay

Silent video · text on screen only

The text IS the entire ad. Copywriting carries everything — the visual sets emotional context, the text does the selling.

Best for: scenic visuals, screen recordings, dramatic footage, category anchors, UGC reactions

Hook text: Full hook (5-20 words) — creates unresolved tension

Body text: Resolves the hook's premise through the product

Hook3s
Body5s
CTA2s
Total~10s

Voiceover + Caption

Person speaks · short caption on screen

ONE CONTINUOUS NARRATION across hook + body. The bridge lives in the voiceover flow — last hook sentence connects to first body sentence.

Best for: UGC talking head, street interviews, confessions, authority staging, story-driven hooks

Hook text: Short caption summary (5-15 words)

Hook VO: What the person says (15-30 words)

Body VO: Continues narrating over product footage (10-20 words)

Speaking pace~2.2 words/sec
Hook VO (15-30 words)7-14s
Body VO (10-20 words)5-9s
CTA2-3s
Total VO words25-50 max
Total duration15-30s (never exceed)

Sora2 Face Clip Rules

  • MAX 4s per face clip — Sora2 can't maintain facial coherence beyond 4 seconds
  • ONE expression per clip — single emotional state, not a transition
  • ONE gaze direction + one micro-variation (anchor + one small movement)
  • Phone glow NEVER sole light source — always describe stronger ambient
  • No phone screen in frame — angled away, face-down, below frame
  • Camera = real TikTok creator — propped on nightstand, dashboard mount, handheld at arm's length

Copy Quality Rules

Hooks create TENSION, not resolution
The hook must leave something unresolved — guilt, curiosity, identity gap. If the tension is already resolved in the hook, there's no reason to keep watching.
BAD: "screen time guilt is gone when they're watching this" (already resolved)
GOOD: "my kids know every Marvel character but can't name a single apostle" (tension: I'm failing)
Body resolves the hook's SPECIFIC premise
The body text is the second beat of the copy, not a product card. It answers the question the hook created.
BAD: "Bible Netflix - unlimited shows" (TV commercial product announcement)
GOOD: "now they binge Bible stories the same way — and actually remember them"
Natural flowing sentences ONLY
How a person talks, texts, or thinks internally. Must pass the "would a real person say this to a friend?" test.
BAD: "No fluff. No filler. Just results." (AI staccato — ChatGPT signature)
GOOD: "I have every Bible app on my phone and I still don't read Scripture"
NO metaphors, NO poetry, NO literary language
Real people don't talk in literary constructs. Specific, concrete, plain language.
BAD: "spiritually starving while sitting in front of a feast"
GOOD: "I scroll for two hours every night and then wonder why I feel empty"
Emotion follows the product
Don't force negative or positive. Faith app = guilt, longing. Candy = craving, nostalgia. Book app = curiosity, intellectual ego. Follow the product's emotional world.
Platform voice matters
TikTok: "ok but why did no one tell me about this" — casual, confessional, lowercase energy.
Meta/IG: "I kept saying I'd start reading my Bible again. It's been two years." — composed but concrete.
NO proxy embarrassment
Never use children, family, friends as the source of shame/guilt. The emotion must come from WITHIN the viewer.
Hook text + visual = ONE UNIT
The visual sets the emotional context, the text articulates it. They must convey the SAME emotion. Never text on a disconnected background.

Known AI Blindspots

Patterns where Claude consistently fails without explicit correction.

  • Sanitizes by default — skips shame, temptation, guilt, lust-adjacent audiences. These are the highest-intent segments. Use implication, not banned words.
  • Thinks in abstract categories — outputs "guilt" as a label instead of a specific visceral moment like "I set my alarm to pray and hit snooze every single time."
  • Avoids negative emotion — leans aspirational when pain converts harder. Jumps to the AFTER state instead of sitting in the BEFORE.
  • Hooks resolve tension instead of creating it — "screen time guilt is gone" states the resolution in the hook. Nothing left for the body. Hooks should be the BEFORE, not the AFTER.
  • Body text defaults to product description — produces "Netflix for Bible content - unlimited shows" (TV commercial) instead of resolving the hook's premise through the product.
  • Staccato AI fragment pattern — "No fluff. No filler. Just results." / "Simple. Clean. Done." Most recognizable AI writing pattern. Instantly breaks authenticity.
  • Over-intellectualizes — adds framework complexity where raw simplicity wins. A hook doesn't need to be clever, it needs to be true.
